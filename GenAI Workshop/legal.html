School Anti-Bullying Policy

I. Purpose

This policy aims to provide a safe and respectful environment for all students by preventing, identifying, and addressing bullying in all forms. It ensures compliance with national and local education regulations and reinforces the school's commitment to a zero-tolerance stance on bullying.

II. Definition of Bullying

Bullying is defined as repeated aggressive behavior that involves a real or perceived power imbalance. It can be:

  • Physical: Hitting, pushing, spitting, or damaging property.
  • Verbal: Name-calling, teasing, threats, or discriminatory remarks.
  • Social/Relational: Exclusion, spreading rumors, or humiliating someone in public.
  • Cyberbullying: Harassment via digital means, including social media, texting, or email.

III. Scope of Policy

This policy applies to:

  • All students, staff, and faculty within school premises.
  • School-sponsored activities, events, and transportation.
  • Online interactions involving school community members.

IV. Prevention Strategies

  • Education & Awareness: Regular sessions on bullying prevention for students, teachers, and parents.
  • Supervision: Increased monitoring in bullying-prone areas like hallways, cafeterias, and online platforms.
  • Student Support Programs: Counseling services and peer mediation opportunities.
  • Parental Engagement: Workshops and open communication channels with guardians.

V. Reporting & Investigation Procedures

  • Reporting: Any student, parent, or staff member may report bullying incidents to a designated school authority.
  • Investigation: The school administration will conduct a confidential and timely investigation.
  • Resolution: If bullying is confirmed, disciplinary action will be taken, and appropriate support will be provided to both the victim and the perpetrator.

VI. Disciplinary Actions

Consequences for bullying may include, but are not limited to:

  • Verbal/written warnings.
  • Parental notification and involvement.
  • Counseling sessions for the perpetrator.
  • Detention or suspension (depending on severity).
  • Expulsion in extreme cases.
  • Referral to law enforcement if necessary.

VII. Support for Victims

The school shall provide:

  • Psychological counseling.
  • Safe spaces for students who feel threatened.
  • Academic support if bullying affects a student’s performance.

VIII. Monitoring & Policy Review

  • This policy shall be reviewed annually.
  • Feedback from students, parents, and faculty will be considered in updates.
  • School administrators shall provide periodic reports on bullying incidents and resolutions.
